St. Sebastian was a 3rd century Christian martyr and saint.
He was born in Narbonne, Gaul (modern-day France).
He was a captain in the Roman army and was known for his bravery and devotion to Christianity.
He was sentenced to death by Emperor Diocletian for being a Christian.
He was tied to a tree and shot with arrows, but miraculously survived.
He was eventually clubbed to death and became a martyr for the Christian faith.
St. Sebastian is one of the 14 Holy Helpers, a group of saints venerated together in the Catholic Church.
He is the patron saint of soldiers, athletes, and those suffering from plagues or infectious diseases.
St. Sebastian is often depicted in art as a youth tied to a tree and shot with arrows.
